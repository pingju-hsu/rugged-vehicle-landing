FAQ

Rugged Vehicle Computing Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from fleet operators, procurement teams, and system integrators — pre-answered for fast reference.

Cold chain deployments require three specific certifications — MIL-STD-810G for vibration and shock, IP-rated enclosures for moisture ingress protection, and operating temperature ranges extending to −30°C or lower. Advantech TREK platforms for cold chain include integrated temperature sensor inputs, 4G/5G redundant connectivity for continuous telemetry, and pre-validated integration paths with major WMS and TMS platforms. Selection should start with your lowest operating temperature and worst-case vibration profile, then match certifications accordingly.

The best VMT for warehouse forklift operations combines sunlight-readable displays, glove-compatible touchscreens, and shock-resistant mounting hardware rated for constant vibration. Advantech's VMT series meets ANSI B56.11 forklift mounting standards and supports both legacy serial and modern Ethernet WMS protocols through a single terminal. Screen size selection depends on workflow complexity — 10-inch for pick-and-pack, 12-inch for complex receiving workflows.

Advantech TREK in-vehicle platforms designated for public transit and rail carry EN 50155 compliance for rolling stock electronics, E-Mark approval for automotive electromagnetic compatibility, and ISO 7637-2 for in-vehicle voltage transient immunity. Specific certifications vary by SKU — consult the Advantech selection guide or engage a regional FAE for verification on your target deployment profile.

AI-powered driver monitoring systems (DMS) use in-cab cameras and edge inference to detect fatigue, distraction, and policy violations in real time. By issuing in-cab alerts and logging verifiable incident data with timestamps, DMS platforms deliver documented due-diligence evidence that reduces insurance exposure and at-fault liability in commercial fleet operations. Advantech TREK DMS platforms run inference locally on the vehicle, so alerting continues even when cellular connectivity is lost.

Advantech TREK products are built on industrial-grade components with long-term supply commitments — typically spanning multiple years beyond consumer-grade alternatives. This supply longevity, combined with substantially lower failure rates in field conditions, lowers the amortized 5-year total cost of ownership compared to consumer tablets that require replacement every 18–24 months. Procurement teams should model TCO across replacement cycles, integration labor, and downtime cost — not just unit price.
